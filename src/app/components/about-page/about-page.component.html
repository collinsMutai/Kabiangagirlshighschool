Our History

Kabianga Girls High School started as a community harambee mixed boarding and day School. It was founded in 1982 and aimed at enhancing the education of youths at the secondary level of education. On this foundation, its aim was to absorb pupils from the neighbourhood only to collapse in the year 1987. Later in the year 1989, the Kabianga Girls (then Kapcheluch Day) opened its gates for boys and girls once more.

Kabianga Girls is situated on on 2.8 acre of land 2km from Kabianga market, in Kabianga location, Sosiot division of Belgut constituency. The school has been in existence for 33 years during which, many students have been able to benefit academically, spiritually and socially because qualitative academic provision for boys and girls has been lacking.

The school started as a single stream of about 120 students and has grown to three streams of approximately 700 students. The school is sponsored by the AIC Church, the current board of management chair is Joyce Chumo, and the PA chair Rev. Edward Kirui. The school has 22 TSC teachers, 11 BOM and 25 support staff.

The school infrastructure is not sufficient for the growing number of students population, and is currently undertaking construction of a 3 storey boarding facility which will accommodate the growing students population. The government has committed to support the school infrastructure development to meet the competency Based Curriculum (CBC) requirements which will enable the school to be a top performer in the region.
